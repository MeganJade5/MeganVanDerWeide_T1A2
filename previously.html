Previously

My industry experience began as a professional in the backstage entertainment industry who specialised in performer flying and automation. The best way I explain my job to my parents and friends (outside of the industry) is, I make things ie. walls and floors or people move with computers. Over my 10 years of exposure in the entertainment industry I worked my way up in the industry from a stage hand, to a manager and to a high risk rigger. I was one of the few women in automation/rigging in my industry, a job that was seen predominantly for men.

This profession allowed for me to practice my craft all around the world including touring large commercial productions and many years sailing on cruiseship with Royal Carribbean. I stayed and progressed in these roles even when the odds were against me. Having my opinions shut down and questioned created motivation for me to push even harder to ensure safety and efficiency in my workplace.  I will use this leadership mindset and passion to progress myself as a figurehead in the space to endorse fellow young women to embark on a similar career path.
